Faith & Harmony

Where Many Paths Meet

Mangalore is one of India's finest examples of interfaith coexistence. Temples thousands of years old, mosques with rich architectural heritage, and colonial-era churches all stand within miles of one another — each a community's centre of life and belonging.

🛕 Temple

Kadri Manjunath Temple

One of the oldest and most significant temples in Karnataka, the Kadri complex is home to a remarkable 10th-century bronze idol of Lokeshvara — considered the oldest dated and inscribed bronze image in South India. The terraced gardens, sacred tank, and meditation caves carved into the hillside create an atmosphere of profound peace. Dedicated to Lord Manjunatha (a form of Shiva), the temple hosts grand celebrations during Shivaratri.

📍 Kadri Hill, Mangalore
🕐 6:00 AM – 1:00 PM, 4:00 – 8:00 PM
🎪 Shivaratri Festival (Feb/Mar)
🛕 Temple

Mangaladevi Temple

The temple that gave Mangalore its very name — "Mangaladevi" became "Mangaluru." Built in the 9th century and believed to have been consecrated by Adi Shankaracharya himself, this temple dedicated to Goddess Mangaladevi sits on a hilltop and commands spiritual authority over the entire city. The annual Navarathri Brahmakalasha festival here draws devotees from across the region.

📍 Bolar, Mangalore
🕐 5:30 AM – 1:00 PM, 5:00 – 8:30 PM
🎪 Navarathri Brahmakalasha

Zeenath Baksh Mosque

One of Mangalore's most revered mosques, Zeenath Baksh (meaning "Bestower of Beauty") is an architectural gem combining traditional South Indian mosque design with ornate detailing. Located in the heart of the old city, it serves the Beary Muslim community and is particularly vibrant during Ramadan and Eid al-Fitr, when the surrounding streets fill with festive food stalls and night markets.

📍 Bunder, Old Mangalore City
🕐 Open for prayer times
🎪 Vibrant during Ramadan & Eid

⛪ Church

St. Aloysius Chapel

Built in 1880 by the Society of Jesus, St. Aloysius Chapel is Mangalore's most celebrated architectural treasure. The entire interior — ceiling, walls, and arches — is covered in 53 vibrant frescoes painted by Italian Jesuit Brother Antonio Moscheni between 1899 and 1900. Depicting scenes from the Old and New Testament, the paintings are executed in extraordinary detail and have earned the chapel its reputation as the "Sistine Chapel of Mangalore." Karnataka's most visited church.

📍 Lighthouse Hill, Mangalore
🕐 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (Mon–Sat)
💰 Free entry
⛪ Cathedral

Rosario Cathedral

The mother church of the Diocese of Mangalore, Rosario Cathedral is an imposing Gothic-style structure overlooking the Netravathi river. Founded in 1526 by the Portuguese, rebuilt and expanded over centuries, the cathedral is the spiritual centre of Mangalore's Konkani Catholic community. Its annual Monte Mariano feast in September draws thousands of pilgrims, while Christmas and Easter are celebrated with grand processions and candlelight.

📍 Bolar, Mangalore
🕐 Open daily for Mass
🎪 Monte Mariano Feast (September)

One City, Many Faiths

For centuries, Mangalore has been a beacon of communal harmony. The city's Hindus, Muslims, and Christians share neighbourhoods, celebrate each other's festivals, and have built one of India's most enduring examples of peaceful coexistence.
