Coastal Karnataka Kitchen

A Cuisine Unlike Any Other

Mangalorean food is a glorious collision of coastal abundance and spice-route heritage. Coconut in every form — milk, grated, oil — is the soul of the kitchen, paired with fiery red chillies from Byadgi and fresh catch from the Arabian Sea.

🥥

Coconut-Forward

Fresh coconut forms the base of chutneys, curries, and even desserts in every Mangalorean kitchen.

🌶️

Richly Spiced

Byadgi chillies, pepper, and fresh coriander combine for a heat that is flavourful rather than overwhelming.

🐟

Seafood Soul

From sardines to kingfish, pomfret to tiger prawns — the sea provides and Mangalore cooks with reverence.

Featured Speciality

Iconic Dish

Mangalorean Fish Curry

There is no dish more emblematic of Mangalore's identity than its fish curry — a deep-red, tangy gravy made with Byadgi chillies, fresh coconut milk, and a generous dose of kudampuli (Garcinia cambogia) for that signature tartness. Simmered with seer fish or mackerel and served with boiled rice, it is the ultimate expression of coastal Karnataka on a plate.

  • Made with Byadgi chillies & coconut milk
  • Soured with kudampuli (Kodampuli/Gamboge)
  • Best eaten with neer dosa or boiled rice

Must Try

Authentic Mangalorean Dishes

Breakfast

Neer Dosa

Paper-thin, ghostly white crepes made from rice batter with no fermentation — neer ("water" in Tulu) dosas are steamed on a flat pan and served rolled or folded alongside coconut chutney, chicken curry, or jaggery. Light, delicate, and utterly addictive.

Vegetarian friendly Gluten free Light
Signature

Kori Rotti

The undisputed king of Tulu Nadu cuisine — crispy, wafer-like rice crackers (rotti) soaked in a fiery chicken (kori) coconut curry. The crackers soften just enough to absorb the curry while retaining a slight crunch. A dish of extraordinary, comforting depth.

Non-vegetarian Spicy Traditional
Breakfast

Mangalore Buns

Soft, pillowy deep-fried bread made from ripe banana, maida, and a touch of sugar — slightly sweet, beautifully puffy, and best eaten hot with coconut chutney and sambar. A quintessential Mangalorean breakfast item you will find nowhere else quite like this.

Vegetarian Sweet & savoury Deep fried
Snack

Goli Baje

Golden, crunchy fritters made from maida, sour curd, green chilli, ginger, and curry leaves — fried to crispy perfection and served piping hot with coconut chutney. Also known as Mysore Bajji, Goli Baje is the definitive Mangalorean tea-time snack.

Vegetarian Crispy Street food
Must Try

Chicken Sukka

A dry-preparation chicken dish cooked with freshly grated coconut, aromatic spices, and curry leaves — sukka means "dry" and this dish delivers concentrated, smoky flavour in every bite. Served as a side at weddings and celebrations, it is pure Tulu cuisine royalty.

Non-vegetarian Dry preparation Festive
