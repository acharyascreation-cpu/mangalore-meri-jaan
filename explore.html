Sun, Sand & Sea

Mangalore's Beaches

The coastline stretching north and south of the city offers golden sands, vivid sunsets, and a laid-back rhythm that washes all worries away.

Most Popular

Panambur Beach

Mangalore's most visited beach stretches wide with clean sands and a festive atmosphere. Home to the annual International Kite Festival, it also offers water sports like jet skiing and banana boat rides. The stunning sunsets here are legendary among locals.

  • International Kite Festival (December)
  • Water sports & camel rides
  • Beach food stalls & bhajji shops
  • Iconic sunset views over the sea
Peaceful Escape

Tannirbhavi Beach

Reachable only by ferry across the Gurpur river mouth, Tannirbhavi is Mangalore's secluded gem. The journey itself — a short, scenic boat ride — is part of the charm. Casuarina groves line the beach and the waters are calm and pristine, perfect for quiet reflection.

  • Scenic ferry ride from Panambur
  • Casuarina shaded picnic spots
  • Calm, swimmable waters
  • Almost always uncrowded
Rocky & Wild

Someshwara Beach

South of the city, Someshwara Beach captivates with its dramatic rocky outcrops, ancient Someshwara temple perched at the shore, and roaring waves. The unique basalt rock formations create natural pools perfect for rock-hopping at low tide. A favourite for photographers.

  • Ancient Someshwara Shiva Temple
  • Dramatic basalt rock formations
  • Natural tidal pools
  • Spectacular wave action in monsoon

Landmarks & History

Must-Visit Tourist Places

From colonial masterpieces to ancient temples, Mangalore's landmarks carry the weight of centuries and the beauty of artisans long past.

Colonial Heritage

St. Aloysius Chapel

Built in 1880 by the Jesuit Fathers, this Baroque chapel's interior is entirely covered in breathtaking frescoes painted by Brother Antonio Moscheni. Often called the "Sistine Chapel of Mangalore," it is Karnataka's most visited church.

Open: 9 AM – 5 PM Free Entry
Sacred Temple

Kadri Manjunath Temple

One of Karnataka's most ancient temples, the Kadri complex houses a remarkable 10th-century bronze idol of Lokeshvara — the oldest inscribed bronze statue in India. Terraced tank gardens, Buddhist sculptures, and serene ghats make this a living museum.

Puja: 6 AM & 6 PM Free Entry
Historical Fort

Sultan Battery

Built by Hyder Ali and fortified by Tipu Sultan in the 18th century, this watchtower on the banks of the Gurupur river commanded the approach to the port. Three centuries later it still stands sentinel, framed by golden-hour light and mangrove-lined banks.

Best at Sunrise Free Entry
