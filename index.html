About the City

Where the Sea Meets Culture

Mangalore — or Kudla as locals lovingly call it — sits at the confluence of the Netravathi and Gurpur rivers along the Arabian Sea. A port city of remarkable diversity, it is home to Tulu, Kannada, Konkani, and Beary communities, each contributing a rich tapestry of festivals, cuisine, and traditions.

From the golden sweep of Panambur Beach to the frescoed ceilings of St. Aloysius Chapel, from the fierce rhythms of Yakshagana to the quiet sanctity of Kadri Temple — Mangalore is a city that surprises, delights, and lingers long in memory.

Living Traditions

Arts & Culture

Mangalore's cultural heritage is alive — performed in temples, on village grounds, and under monsoon skies. These art forms have been passed down for centuries.

Spirit Worship

Bhoota Kola

A sacred ritual of the Tulu-speaking people where spirit mediums — adorned in elaborate costumes — channel ancestral spirits to deliver oracles and settle disputes. Performed primarily between December and May.

Traditional Theatre

Yakshagana

A night-long theatrical form combining music, dance, and drama to narrate epic tales from the Mahabharata and Ramayana. Artists in towering headgear and vivid face paint perform till dawn.

Harvest Festival

Pili Nalike

The iconic Tiger Dance of Mangalore, performed during Navaratri with bodies painted in vivid tiger stripes. Groups of dancers move through the streets in a joyful, energetic procession that draws huge crowds.

Traditional Sport

Kambala

The ancient buffalo race of coastal Karnataka — a celebration of the farming community's bond with their animals. Pairs of buffaloes race through paddy fields to the thunderous cheers of thousands of spectators gathered on the banks.

Unity in Diversity

A City of Many Faiths

Mangalore has long been a model of communal harmony, where temples, mosques, and churches stand side by side and festivals are celebrated across communities.

🛕

Hindu Heritage

Ancient temples, vibrant Navaratri and Diwali celebrations, Yakshagana performances, and the sacred Kudla traditions of the Tulu people.

🕌

Muslim Culture

The Beary community's unique Tulu-Arabic culture, historic mosques, vibrant Eid celebrations, and the world-famous Mangalore biryani traditions.

⛪

Christian Legacy

Historic Gothic and Baroque churches, the Konkani-Catholic community's rich folklore, Monte Mariano pilgrimage, and grand Christmas festivities.
